## Syllabus

**CHAOSMOSIS - Sonoplastic Pluriverse & Polyphonic: Inoculate (M)other Languages** 

Short course description, including a list of the general contents that the program will cover. Maximum extension of 6 lines. 

Short-form artistic–pedagogical 3-day practice-based where participants enter the instructor’s working  methodologies and learn through immersion, collective doing, and exposure. This course is an intensive  sonoplastic laboratory grounded in radical pedagogical practices of DIY–DIWO–DIT (Do-It-Yourself, Do-It With-Others, Do-It-Together) as creative responses to ecological, social, and nature-culture metabolisms 
of emergencies. The course explores sound, image, matter, and embodiment as interconnected co creation within human, non-human and more-than-human environments and care protocols, with  particular attention to liminal necropolitical urban–rural socio-ecological contexts and relationships shaped by tecnosphere. 

Through situated experimentation and collective research-making, students work across acoustic  ecology, video art, microscopy, and biomaterials to investigate how sound, images, and living matter as  sentient sediments–sentiments, memory, relation of care. The laboratory integrates perspectives from soft sciences, hard sciences, and childlike wonder artistic practices, encouraging embodied learning, curiosity, and critical reflection by doing-in-action. 

Students develop monographic sonoplastic experiments by collecting, recording, editing, and translating  bodily and field materials into multisensory co-created compositions. These experiments culminate in a  collective sonoplastic chaosmotic pluriverse and polyphonic happening contaminated by the conceptual  
body-grammar worked in class and a self-published memory-documentation, which together function as a situated archive for future storytelling and knowledge transmission of care. 

**Course Contents**: 
1. **Conceptual grounding - Chaosmosis and the Otherness**: Co-creating within sacrificed  more-than-human pluriverse and necropolitical urban–rural socio-ecological zones and  hydrofeminism (soft sciences) 
2. **Care protocols and Reflection-through-doing as DIY–DIWO–DIT Metabolisms of  Emergency and Resistance for future storytelling**: implementation, more-than-human  protocols with institutions and entities economies (care as piracy and piracy as  care) 
3. **Embodied experimentation - Situated Sonoplastic Plural and Polyphonic Toolkits**: Acoustic ecologies / video art / botanics / geology / microbiology / Biomaterials /  concrete-storytelling / self-editions-publications (hard sciences and art brut) 4. Collective making - The Happening and Brutalism as DIY-DIWO-DIT intervention  and memory-documentation transmission – Chaosmosis: Inoculating (M)other  languages (eco-ritual) 



## Learning Objectives
List of the principal learning objectives that students are expected to achieve once they finish the course,  and how they will be developed. 
The principal learning objectives that students are expected to achieve upon completion of the course  are: 

1. Activate and explore DIY–DIWO–DIT methodologies as metabolisms of emergency and  resistance, developing storytelling practices that reimagine more-than-human futures of care and  repair through embodiment, experimentation, commitment, and co-creation as a community based process. 
2. Co-create experiments using situated sonoplastic toolkits by demonstrating, producing,  articulating, and documenting work that integrates acoustic ecologies, video art, microscopy, 
botanics, geology, microbiology, sound poetry, performance, and biomaterials, critically exploring  the interconnection of multiple intelligences across soft sciences, hard sciences, biomimesis, and  art brut as collective pedagogical practices. 
3. Develop concrete creative and didactical forms of embodied, monographic research-making and  pedagogical intervention, disrupting dominant nature–culture narratives through brutalist and  chaosmotic processes within specific socio-ecological and necropolitical contexts. 
4. Produce a collective sonoplastic outcome culminating in a happening-performance eco-ritual and  a collectively published memory-documentation that functions as a situated archive for future  storytelling and transgenerational knowledge transmission. 
5. Critically reflect on pedagogy as contamination and cross-pollination, understanding learning-by doing as a vulnerable process that reconfigures plural and polyphonic nature–culture  relations among human, non-human, and more-than-human actors toward care, defense,  and response-ability within sacrificed urban–rural socio-ecological zones and more-than-human  chaosmotic pluriverse. 



**Keywords: Sonoplastic Tools; Video Art and Acoustic Ecologies; More-than-Human Relations; Situated and Field Based Practice; Community Practices; DIY–DIWO–DIT Metabolisms; Radical Pedagogy; Experiential Learning and Embodiment; Biomaterials; Care-Defense and Repair**

**Skills**: Field research; site-responsive practice; audio–video documentation; biomaterial collection;  autonomous inquiry; spatial listening; situated critical reflection.

## Metodological Strategies

1. **Participatory Situated DIY–DIWO–DIT Co-Creation and Action** 
Learning unfolds through site-responsive, collective design action in real contexts, emphasizing  experimentation, ethical engagement, and shared authorship as forms of situated knowledge production.
**Key Competences**: Site-responsive inquiry · Transdisciplinary experimentation · DIY–DIWO–DIT co creation · Peer learning · Ethical engagement · Context-based practice 
2. **Multisensory Prototypes — Embodied Sonoplastic & Laboratory Experimentation**
Research–make–educate practices develop multisensory design prototypes integrating sound, image,  biological matter, and sensory perception across soft sciences, hard sciences, biomimicry, and art brut  through hands-on experimentation.
**Key Competences**: Sound recording · Video experimentation · Editing processes · Acoustic ecologies ·  Microbiological processes · Biomaterials · Ecological observation · DIY and open-source tools 
3. **Care Design — Radical Pedagogy and Critical Reflection** 
Pedagogy operates as a care-based ethical design framework, enabling reflexive contamination and  cross-pollination to question dominant narratives and support collective transformation.
**Key Competences**: Care-based ethics · Knowledge critique · Response-ability · Embodied and sensory  learning · Affect-based inquiry · Perceptual attunement · Bodily listening 
4. **Co-creation as a more-than-human Social Processes — Translation, Documentation, and  Collective Transmission** 
Practices and processes embodiments are translated into performative actions and experimental  documentation, generating zines, archives, and happenings as tools for memory, storytelling, and long term knowledge transmission. 
**Key Competences**: Performative and communicative practices · Zine-making · Audiovisual archiving ·  Process-based documentation · Shared storytelling · Memory transmission

## Evaluation Strategies 

**Evaluation criteria prioritize process, care, experimentation, and relational impact learning over individual technical mastery or product-based outcomes alone, aligned  with Radical Pedagogy.**

**Evaluation Criteria** 
**Situated Engagement & Care** (Formative + Summative | Class and Field Activities) Co-working responsibly within specific socio-ecological contexts and practicing care with human and  more-than-human co-creators. 

**Experimentation & Embodiment** (Formative + Summative | Laboratory Research) Active, hands-on sonoplastic experimentation through bodily listening, material exploration, and sensory based research. 

**Collaboration & DIY–DIWO–DIT Processes** (Formative | Process-Based Making) Participation in collective making, self-organization, knowledge sharing, and the transmission of practices. 

**Critical Reflection** (Summative | Hypothesis Testing and Retro-Feedback) Ability to reflect on learning as contamination, question dominant narratives, and articulate personal and  collective transformation. 

**Storytelling & Documentation** (Summative | Happening and Sonographic Publication) Creation of a coherent sonoplastic outcome and meaningful documentation for future transmission. 

**Assessment emphasizes learning processes, ethical engagement, and collective  responsibility rather than individual technical mastery alone.** 
● Participation & Care Practices: 30% 
● Experimental Process (prototypes as evolving research): 25% 
● Personal & Collective Reflections: 25% 
● Self-Assessment & Peer Feedback: 20%
