    Syllabus⇝

    CHAOSMOSIS - Sonoplastic Pluriverse & Polyphonic: Inoculate (M)other Languages

    Short course description, including a list of the general contents that the program will cover. Maximum extension of 6 lines.

    Short-form artistic–pedagogical 3-day practice-based where participants enter the instructor’s working methodologies and learn through immersion, collective doing, and exposure. This course is an intensive sonoplastic laboratory grounded in radical pedagogical practices of DIY–DIWO–DIT (Do-It-Yourself, Do-It With-Others, Do-It-Together) as creative responses to ecological, social, and nature-culture metabolisms of emergencies. The course explores sound, image, matter, and embodiment as interconnected co creation within human, non-human and more-than-human environments and care protocols, with particular attention to liminal necropolitical urban–rural socio-ecological contexts and relationships shaped by tecnosphere.

    Through situated experimentation and collective research-making, students work across acoustic ecology, video art, microscopy, and biomaterials to investigate how sound, images, and living matter as sentient sediments–sentiments, memory, relation of care. The laboratory integrates perspectives from soft sciences, hard sciences, and childlike wonder artistic practices, encouraging embodied learning, curiosity, and critical reflection by doing-in-action.

    Students develop monographic sonoplastic experiments by collecting, recording, editing, and translating bodily and field materials into multisensory co-created compositions. These experiments culminate in a collective sonoplastic chaosmotic pluriverse and polyphonic happening contaminated by the conceptual
    body-grammar worked in class and a self-published memory-documentation, which together function as a situated archive for future storytelling and knowledge transmission of care.

    Course Contents: 1. Conceptual grounding - Chaosmosis and the Otherness: Co-creating within sacrificed more-than-human pluriverse and necropolitical urban–rural socio-ecological zones and hydrofeminism (soft sciences) 2. Care protocols and Reflection-through-doing as DIY–DIWO–DIT Metabolisms of Emergency and Resistance for future storytelling: implementation, more-than-human protocols with institutions and entities economies (care as piracy and piracy as care) 3. Embodied experimentation - Situated Sonoplastic Plural and Polyphonic Toolkits: Acoustic ecologies / video art / botanics / geology / microbiology / Biomaterials / concrete-storytelling / self-editions-publications (hard sciences and art brut) 4. Collective making - The Happening and Brutalism as DIY-DIWO-DIT intervention and memory-documentation transmission – Chaosmosis: Inoculating (M)other languages (eco-ritual)

    Learning Objectives⇝

    List of the principal learning objectives that students are expected to achieve once they finish the course, and how they will be developed. The principal learning objectives that students are expected to achieve upon completion of the course are:

    1. Activate and explore DIY–DIWO–DIT methodologies as metabolisms of emergency and resistance, developing storytelling practices that reimagine more-than-human futures of care and repair through embodiment, experimentation, commitment, and co-creation as a community based process.
    2. Co-create experiments using situated sonoplastic toolkits by demonstrating, producing, articulating, and documenting work that integrates acoustic ecologies, video art, microscopy, botanics, geology, microbiology, sound poetry, performance, and biomaterials, critically exploring the interconnection of multiple intelligences across soft sciences, hard sciences, biomimesis, and art brut as collective pedagogical practices.
    3. Develop concrete creative and didactical forms of embodied, monographic research-making and pedagogical intervention, disrupting dominant nature–culture narratives through brutalist and chaosmotic processes within specific socio-ecological and necropolitical contexts.
    4. Produce a collective sonoplastic outcome culminating in a happening-performance eco-ritual and a collectively published memory-documentation that functions as a situated archive for future storytelling and transgenerational knowledge transmission.
    5. Critically reflect on pedagogy as contamination and cross-pollination, understanding learning-by doing as a vulnerable process that reconfigures plural and polyphonic nature–culture relations among human, non-human, and more-than-human actors toward care, defense, and response-ability within sacrificed urban–rural socio-ecological zones and more-than-human chaosmotic pluriverse.

    Keywords: Sonoplastic Tools; Video Art and Acoustic Ecologies; More-than-Human Relations; Situated and Field Based Practice; Community Practices; DIY–DIWO–DIT Metabolisms; Radical Pedagogy; Experiential Learning and Embodiment; Biomaterials; Care-Defense and Repair

    Schedule⇝

    Pedagogical-Phases: Attunement → Situated Experimentation → Translation → Collective Transmission

    Day 1 - 9:30-11:30⇝

    AOUCH LAB — Tactability / Sense of Existence I & II Tasks / Actions: ● Participate in sensory and group attunement exercises. ● Complete the Archive of the Invisible Things questionnaire. ● Explore laboratory tools and transdisciplinary materials (sound, video, microscopy, biomaterials, zines) interacting with sonoplastic references and short case studies. ● Learn and discuss human and more-than-human care protocols. Skills: Sensory attunement; embodied perception; laboratory exploration; transdisciplinary tool literacy; ethical awareness; more-than-human care; critical observation; collective learning.

    Day 1 - 11:45-13:30⇝

    SONOPLASTIC TOOLKIT — Fieldwork Preparation Tasks / Actions: ● Learn basic principles of acoustic ecology and sound recording. ● Learn experimental video practices using mobile devices. ● Receive instructions and ethical guidelines for fieldwork. ● Learn protocols for collecting sound, video, and biomaterial samples. Skills: Acoustic ecology fundamentals; sound recording; experimental mobile video; sampling protocols; technical preparation; field ethics; problem-solving; risk awareness.

    Day 1 - Onward (Independent Work)⇝

    LET’S GET LOST — Cartography of Intimate Lost Places Tasks / Actions: ● Conduct a field search in an urban–rural location. ● Record sound and video. ● Collect biomaterial samples when appropriate. ● Document bodily and sensory experiences. Skills: Field research; site-responsive practice; audio–video documentation; biomaterial collection; autonomous inquiry; spatial listening; situated critical reflection.

    Day 2 - 9:30-10:30⇝

    PEDAGOGICAL CONTAMINATION & CRITICAL EMBODIMENT Tasks / Actions: ● Review key concepts collectively. ● Write an automatic poem based on the field experience. ● Share and discuss texts and reflections. Skills: Critical reflection; automatic writing; vocal reading; conceptual synthesis; embodied cognition; dialogical exchange; reflexive pedagogy.

    Day 2 - 10:45-13:30⇝

    CHAOSMOSIS IN ONE DROP — Laboratory & MASH-UP Tasks / Actions: ● Build Petri dishes using collected biological samples. ● Observe and document biological processes with microscopy. ● Edit a 49-second sound experiment. ● Edit a 49-second video experiment. ● Select one image and one sound file for publication. Skills: Basic microbiology; laboratory documentation; microscopy observation; sound editing; video editing; audiovisual composition; material experimentation; iterative research.

    Day 3 - 9:30-11:30⇝

    CUT & GLUE ARCHIVE — GLÁNDULAS SYNTAX III Tasks / Actions: ● Create a collective sonographic zine. ● Combine one image, one text, and one sound per participant. ● Assemble the master zine using collage and handwriting. ● Upload sound and video files. ● Prepare the space and equipment for the happening. Skills: DIY publishing; collage techniques; sonographic archiving; collective authorship; editorial decision-making; analog–digital translation; memory construction.

    Day 3 - 11:45-13:30⇝

    MORE-THAN-HUMAN BODY LAB & THE CHAOSMOSIS SONOPLASTIC ACT Tasks / Actions: ● Participate in the Body Lab with guest artists Marina Barsey Janer & Isil Sol Vil ● DATA-ACTION-DATA : Perform in the collective sonoplastic happening. ● Engage in collective reflection and pedagogical contamination. Skills: Performance practice; live audiovisual mixing; vocal experimentation; embodied interaction; collaboration; systems thinking; praxis-based evaluation; collective transmission.

    Metodological Strategies⇝

    1. Participatory Situated DIY–DIWO–DIT Co-Creation and Action Learning unfolds through site-responsive, collective design action in real contexts, emphasizing experimentation, ethical engagement, and shared authorship as forms of situated knowledge production. Key Competences: Site-responsive inquiry · Transdisciplinary experimentation · DIY–DIWO–DIT co creation · Peer learning · Ethical engagement · Context-based practice
    2. Multisensory Prototypes — Embodied Sonoplastic & Laboratory Experimentation Research–make–educate practices develop multisensory design prototypes integrating sound, image, biological matter, and sensory perception across soft sciences, hard sciences, biomimicry, and art brut through hands-on experimentation. Key Competences: Sound recording · Video experimentation · Editing processes · Acoustic ecologies · Microbiological processes · Biomaterials · Ecological observation · DIY and open-source tools
    3. Care Design — Radical Pedagogy and Critical Reflection Pedagogy operates as a care-based ethical design framework, enabling reflexive contamination and cross-pollination to question dominant narratives and support collective transformation. Key Competences: Care-based ethics · Knowledge critique · Response-ability · Embodied and sensory learning · Affect-based inquiry · Perceptual attunement · Bodily listening
    4. Co-creation as a more-than-human Social Processes — Translation, Documentation, and Collective Transmission Practices and processes embodiments are translated into performative actions and experimental documentation, generating zines, archives, and happenings as tools for memory, storytelling, and long term knowledge transmission. Key Competences: Performative and communicative practices · Zine-making · Audiovisual archiving · Process-based documentation · Shared storytelling · Memory transmission

    Deliverables⇝

    ARTIFACTS Experiment 1 — Situated Sonoplastic Audio Experiment Recorded and edited sound experiment (49 seconds).

    Experiment 2 — Situated Visual Experiment Recorded and edited visual experiment (49 seconds).

    Experiment 3 — Material / Microbiological Exploration (Making / Production) Production of two Petri dishes (microbiology and microbiome): The Relic.

    Experiment 4 — Textual Production Written text grounded in the course’s conceptual framework.

    Experiment 5 — Performance lab and Live Happening Sonoplastic Incision: a live performance intersecting audiovisual composition and Vocalog. A collective mix of the 49-second recorded audiovisual experiments (25 creators × 2 loops ≈ 25-minute piece), combined with live microscopy experimentation and readings as a process of contamination.

    Experiment 6 — Sonographic Zine (Collective Publication) Graphic extraction from the visual action and textual material combined with recorded sound, functioning as memory documentation and a situated archive to be self-published.

    Evaluation Strategies⇝

    Evaluation criteria prioritize process, care, experimentation, and relational impact learning over individual technical mastery or product-based outcomes alone, aligned with Radical Pedagogy.

    Evaluation Criteria Situated Engagement & Care (Formative + Summative | Class and Field Activities) Co-working responsibly within specific socio-ecological contexts and practicing care with human and more-than-human co-creators.

    Experimentation & Embodiment (Formative + Summative | Laboratory Research) Active, hands-on sonoplastic experimentation through bodily listening, material exploration, and sensory based research.

    Collaboration & DIY–DIWO–DIT Processes (Formative | Process-Based Making) Participation in collective making, self-organization, knowledge sharing, and the transmission of practices.

    Critical Reflection (Summative | Hypothesis Testing and Retro-Feedback) Ability to reflect on learning as contamination, question dominant narratives, and articulate personal and collective transformation.

    Storytelling & Documentation (Summative | Happening and Sonographic Publication) Creation of a coherent sonoplastic outcome and meaningful documentation for future transmission.

    Grading Method⇝

    Assessment emphasizes learning processes, ethical engagement, and collective responsibility rather than individual technical mastery alone.

    Percentage Description
    30% Participation & Care Practices
    25% Experimental Process (prototypes as evolving research)
    25% Personal & Collective Reflections
    20% Self-Assessment & Peer Feedback
